"I'M THE ONLY ONE THAT'S SEARCHING."

This is a voice memo from when I first wrote the chorus for "Soak" in April of 2019. The verses went through many revisions both melodically and lyrically, but this chorus always felt right to me and stayed the same from when I intially wrote it. It was one of those freakish moments where everything fell into place and I was just singing what I instinctually felt.

"Soak" was written in a post-concert daze. I wanted to capture the lingering feeling of euphoria that I had – after many years of struggling with mental illness and isolation caused by bullying and exclusion, I felt like immersing myself in music was becoming a sort of beacon of light. While other people my age tried to find highs through substances and extroverted behaviors, I clutched onto music instead. I wanted to drag out the memories, idealize the people I met, and feel like there was magic in every experience – even if I knew deep down that it was all just a coping mechanism and not truly reflective of reality.
